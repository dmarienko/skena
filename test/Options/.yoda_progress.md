## Key Insights Gained 💡

1. **Scaling Principle:** The $1/\sqrt{n}$ factor balances growth ($n$ steps) vs. shrinkage (step size), making variance constant as $n \to \infty$

2. **Fractal/Self-Similar Property:**
   - Time-shifted process $W_{s+t} - W_s$ is also BM (stationarity of increments)
   - Scaled process $\frac{1}{\sqrt{c}}W_{ct}$ is also BM (scale invariance)

3. **BM.3 is Powerful:** Many processes have marginal distributions $N(0,t)$ but fail the conditional independence structure of BM.3

4. **Common Error Learned:** When computing $Var(A - B)$, use $Var(A) + Var(B) - 2Cov(A,B)$ (minus sign!)
